Mapping the Roots of Hunger in Ontario



To understand hunger patterns in Ontario better, this project sought to reveal not only where Ontarioâ€™s hunger needs exist but also their trends in relation to other factors. Intersections between food bank use and socio-economic variables of interest at regional and local scales reveal an array of geographic relationships and patterns previously invisible.


This project supports the research and advocacy efforts of FeedOntario, a non-profit organization working to unite and support local food banks through a vision of ending hunger and poverty across Ontario.

About Our Client

Founded in 1992, FeedOntario (formerly known as the Ontario Association of Food Banks) is a multi-service agency that unites food banks across Ontario. Their vision is to end hunger and poverty across the province. FeedOntario works to ensure the province's food banks are funded properly and provided with necessary food supply. They also promote change through their research efforts and showcase their work in an annual hunger report that supports their advocacy goals.
